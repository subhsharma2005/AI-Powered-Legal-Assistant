# AI-Powered-Legal-Assistant
AI-Powered Legal Assistant: A RAG System for Indian Law

The Indian judicial system is currently overwhelmed, with over 50 million cases pending across various courts. For
law students, legal researchers, and junior advocates, the challenge is not just the volume of data but the
accessibility of it. Legal research involves navigating complex, archaic databases to find relevant precedents and
constitutional interpretations. Current methods require exact keyword matches, often missing relevant case laws
that use different terminology but address the same legal principles. Furthermore, the high cost of proprietary legal
databases creates a barrier to entry for students and independent practitioners.
This project solves the "search friction" problem by building a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system. It
bridges the gap between raw legal data and human inquiry. It is important because it democratizes access to legal
knowledge, allowing users to ask natural language questions
